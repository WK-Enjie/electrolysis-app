🔑 What is Electrolysis?

Electrolysis is the decomposition of an ionic compound (electrolyte) by the passage of direct electric current through it, when it is in molten or aqueous state.

During electrolysis, cations (positive ions) migrate to the cathode (−) where they gain electrons (reduction), and anions (negative ions) migrate to the anode (+) where they lose electrons (oxidation).

💡 Memory tip: AN OX → Anode = Oxidation  |  RED CAT → Reduction = Cathode
